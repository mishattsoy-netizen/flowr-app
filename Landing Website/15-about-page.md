# About Page (`/about`)

The manifesto page. Almost entirely typography — Libertinus Serif doing the heavy lifting, narrow editorial measure (~720px), one Flow Line illustration, no other imagery. A one-person-with-taste page reads as craft, not smallness: no stock photos, no fake "team" grid.

---

## Structure — three movements

### 1. The itch (personal, first person)

A short note from Mikhail. Directional draft:

> Flowr started with a tab problem. My notes were in one app, my whiteboard in another, my tasks in a third, and my AI in a browser tab that knew nothing about any of them. Every day I paid a small tax moving context between tools that refused to talk to each other.
>
> I wanted one quiet place where everything I know lives together — and an AI that actually knows it too.

### 2. The belief

Short serif statements, generous spacing:

> **Your tools should share one memory.**
> **Software should feel calm.**
> **Your second brain belongs to you — on your device, in your control.**
> **An AI is only as useful as the context you let it keep.**

### 3. The practice

How it's built:

> Flowr is built in the open by one person, in beta, shipping every week. Users see the roadmap, vote on it, and propose what's next. When something's rough, the changelog says so; when something ships, you'll hear it from me directly.
>
> — Mikhail

Contact line: email + social links (X/Telegram/Discord as available).

## Design notes

- One Flow Line illustration (a calm variant of the Current)
- Optional small photo of Mikhail — his call; the page works without it
- Nav handles conversion; this page has no CTA block. Trust is the CTA.
