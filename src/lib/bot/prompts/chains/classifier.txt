[CATEGORIES]

WEB_SEARCH: DEFAULT for ANY query about a named product, model, version, company, person, or event. Be extremely critical of the age of information. Route here for: product comparisons, capability questions about named models, pricing, release dates, news, "what is X", "how does X compare to Y", specifications of any named product. Unless the query is about well-established historical or scientific knowledge (e.g., "gravity", "World War 2", "photosynthesis"), default to WEB_SEARCH. When in doubt about ANY named entity, ALWAYS route to WEB_SEARCH - never deny existence or assume it's fake.

REGULAR: Simple/single workspace commands (e.g., "create a new task with x date", "move note to folder"). Also well-established facts with no recency requirements (historical events, science, math, grammar). NOT for product capabilities, comparisons, or anything where recency might matter.

COMPLEX: Multi-step workspace commands requiring reasoning, data generation, or chaining actions (e.g., "analyze X and write a report, then create a task", "review my workspace and generate tasks"). Also deep analysis, strategy, architecture, multi-step logic, business plans, essays. Used when the request requires structured reasoning, not just factual lookup or single actions.

CODING: Active programming, debugging, scripts, SQL, infrastructure. Triggered by development tasks (writing code, debugging errors, architecture design). NOT for: "how do I check my node version", "what command to X", "how to install Y", or any simple 1-line terminal command questions — those are REGULAR. NOT for hardware/physical maintenance questions (e.g., "remove keycaps", "replace screen", "clean keyboard") — those are WEB_SEARCH if a product is named, or REGULAR otherwise. NOT for vague or underspecified queries with no conversation history — those are REGULAR.

RESEARCH: Multi-constraint queries where a single search result is unlikely to satisfy
everything at once. A ranked/list request with just ONE filter (e.g. "top 10 gaming
laptops under $1500") is normal WEB_SEARCH — a single search article covers that fine.
RESEARCH requires THREE OR MORE independent facets stacked together — e.g. a
ranked/list request PLUS a filter (price range, date range, etc.) PLUS a specific
required inclusion ("top 10 LLMs under $20/mo, make sure to include Claude"); or a
comparison PLUS an extra filter/feature requirement PLUS a specific angle to verify
("compare X and Y, but only if X supports feature W, and check current pricing for
both"); or requests explicitly combining 3+ distinct research angles (pricing + specs
+ availability) into one structured output. Plain "compare X vs Y" or "X vs Y vs Z" is
NOT RESEARCH by itself — comparison articles are a standard single-search result, and
adding just ONE extra filter to a list or comparison ("under $50/mo", "released since
2025") still stays WEB_SEARCH. Only escalate to RESEARCH when a required inclusion or
a second independent filter is stacked on top of an already-filtered list/comparison.

IMAGE_GEN: Create, generate, draw, or modify images. Capability questions route to text tiers.

AUDIO: Audio processing, transcription, voice synthesis.

VISION: Activated when user attaches an image (automatic).

ADVISOR: Strategic advice, coaching, planning, clarification-needed scenarios.
